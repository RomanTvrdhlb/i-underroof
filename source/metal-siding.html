Metal siding

Metal siding made of steel is one of the extremely popular solutions for both commercial & residential buildings. It can not just be applied in various ways but also provide a wide range of advantages for houses and their inhabitants.

Exterior metal panels are often made mostly of stainless or galvanized steel, which is a durable & reliable material. The diversity of designs allows creating very different styles.

Pros & Cons of the Covering

The key advantages of metal home siding include:

  • Impressive durability of the material (over 50 years)
  • Toughness of steel
  • Eco-friendliness because steel can be easily recycled & doesn’t contain any harmful substances
  • Absolute resistance to insects & other wildlife
  • Resistance to harsh weather conditions including extreme temperatures, storms, etc.
  • Versatility of the covering
  • The diversity of colors & styles
  • Sound resistance
  • The opportunity for effective insulation
  • Fire resistance
  • Minimal maintenance (it doesn’t require any special treatment).

There’re also a few disadvantages to consider:

  • The risks of rust (especially due to the saltwater influence)
  • The cost of premium steel is higher compared to other materials.

Variations of Covering

The options available with steel covering are numerous. Due to this, every landowner can create a unique design, which will make a house stand out. Still covering might be made with either vertical or horizontal metal siding panels depending on the preferences & the desired effect. Observe the most frequently used types of metal siding for houses:

  • Corrugated

    It’s one of the most appropriate & popular choices for the residential area. The panels are characterized by a ribbed look. Thanks to this, the surface becomes more resistant to significant denting (for instance, caused by hails). The covering is versatile and comes in very different shades & sizes.

  • Ultra Batten

    Thanks to their versatility, these steel siding panels are also extremely popular. They can be set up either horizontally or vertically. In the latest case, the look will become more cozy & farm-like. Another benefit is that it’s possible to use both exposed & concealed fasteners for the installation of the covering.

  • Snap Batten

    Such a solution is able to make any house stand out. The metal siding sheets are created for the vertical installation. They are available with various widths, so homeowners can combine different ones for a more impressive look. The panels are set up primarily with a concealed clip system.

  • Shiplap

    Such covering will create an impression of natural wood. The panels are set up only with hidden fasteners, so the general look is always pleasant & neat. The profiles for such covering might be different. The varieties include flat, single/double stiffener, and variegated assembly.

  • V Groove

    Such variety of exterior metal siding is also perfect for house renovation to create a mesmerizing natural look with the natural texture of wood. The installation is made with hidden fasteners. The surface is smooth & pleasant to the eye.

  • Rustic

    It’s a perfect choice to create a natural look for the house. It’s possible to choose any color you like. Besides, the panels can be set up with either hidden or exposed fasteners. A special look is achieved with painting or weathering.

  • Bare

    It’s one of the variations of rustic-style metal sheet siding. They are mostly made of stainless steel, weathered, or galvanized steel. It might be a good solution to create a vintage look. However, the less painting & finishing are applied, the more prone the material is to external influences. Manufacturers also offer corrugated metal for the creation of the corresponding design.

  • TruCedar

    It’s an ideal alternative to natural cedar that repeats the texture of the wood and provides the highest-quality characteristics at the same time. The material will never wrap or crack. In addition, it’s able to repel water. The steel siding for houses comes with a zinc coating, which adds to its durability.

  • Dutch Lap

    With such panels, overlapping horizontal rows are created. It’s perfect protection from heavy winds & other weather conditions. The sizes of panels are different.

Colors

Steel siding on houses can be painted in any possible color, so the choice of hues is extremely wide. The most popular color variations include:

  • Gray
  • Brown
  • Red cedar
  • Yellow
  • Blue
  • Green
  • Orange, etc.

Many landowners prefer panels with textures that imitate natural and other materials — wood, brick, stone, log, and bars.

Prices

The average cost of steel siding is from 7 USD to 16 USD per square foot. Thus, if the size of a standard house is 1,800 sq. ft., the price for the whole building will be from 12,600 USD to 28,800 USD. The price might greatly vary due to several issues:

  • The amount of work
  • The need for repairs
  • The type & quality of metal
  • The style selected
  • The necessity to remove the old covering
  • The necessity to add insulation to the metal siding on houses
  • The complexity of preparation works.

On average, the price for wood covering varies from 13 USD to 15 USD per square foot. Thus, if the footage of the building is 1,800 sq. ft., the cost will be from 23,400 USD to 27,000 USD for the whole building.

Setting up

The metal siding installation always requires skills & experience. It’s always recommended to rely on professionals in order to guarantee the excellent characteristics and durability of this metal. The installation process usually presupposes the following steps:

1. Preparations

The area should be carefully prepared. The works include the removal of the previous covering, checking the surface for damages, fixing the damages if required (holes & cracks patching), and cleaning the area.

2. Cutting & fitting

The surface should be carefully measured in order to cut the material accurately. Besides, the covering should properly fit the wall.

3. Siding installation

The procedure is fulfilled according to the type of sheet metal siding selected. Only experts know, where it’s necessary to start, which fasteners to use, and other significant details for correct setting up. As an addition, the edges might be trimmed & insulation added.
